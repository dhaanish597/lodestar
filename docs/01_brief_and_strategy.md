## Our problem — PS2: AI-Driven Energy Supply Chain Resilience for Import-Dependent Economies
Build an AI platform that **monitors geopolitical + logistics risk signals continuously**, **models disruption scenarios and downstream economic impact**, and **generates executable procurement-rerouting recommendations** — turning reactive crisis response into an anticipatory, managed process.

Anchor facts from the brief (use in pitch):
- India imports ~**88%** of its crude; **40–45%** transits the **Strait of Hormuz**.
- Strategic Petroleum Reserves ≈ **9.5 days** of national consumption cover.
- Through 2025–26: US–Iran standoff (Brent +8% in a session), renewed Iran-oil sanctions pressure, Persian Gulf maritime incidents, Houthi Red Sea attacks — the supply threat is live.
- A McKinsey analysis: economies without automated rerouting/demand-management took ~**47 days longer** to stabilize after shocks.

### Suggested build areas (from the brief)
Geopolitical Risk Intelligence Agent · Disruption Scenario Modeller · Adaptive Procurement Orchestrator · Strategic Reserve (SPR) Optimisation Agent · Supply-Chain Digital Twin (geospatial).

### Evaluation focus (what they'll actually score on)
Disruption-signal detection lead time & accuracy · quality/executability of procurement alternatives · scenario-model fidelity (**assumptions must be explicit and testable**) · geospatial evidence depth · end-to-end response time from signal → recommendation.

## The ecosystem partner: Octave (know your jury)
Octave is a **Hexagon AB spin-off** (independent public company since May 2026), combining **Asset Lifecycle Intelligence + Safety, Infrastructure & Geospatial + ETQ (quality) + Bricsys (CAD)**. Pillars: **Design · Build · Operate · Protect**. Doctrine: mission-critical software for environments where *"failure is not an option"*; converts fragmented operational data into decisive action; heavy on **geospatial intelligence** and **operational resilience**.

**Why PS2 resonates with Octave:** it's operational-resilience decision-support with a strong geospatial core and a public-sector/critical-infrastructure flavor — squarely in Octave's "Safety, Infrastructure & Geospatial" + asset-lifecycle worldview. Frame our language to echo theirs: *"turning fragmented signals into decisive, anticipatory action across a critical supply chain."*

## Why we chose PS2 (our edge — keep these front of mind)
1. **Real live data on stage.** Unlike most statements, PS2 runs on genuinely live public feeds (real tankers, real Brent, real news). No "faked feed" smell — the strongest possible answer to jury scrutiny.
2. **Thin competition.** Geopolitics + markets + AIS *sounds* intimidating, so few teams attempt it — yet the data is the most accessible. High perceived difficulty + low actual difficulty + near-empty field = arbitrage.
3. **Three themes at once:** Supply Chain + Geospatial + (energy) Industrial — maximal sponsor surface area.
4. **Maximally timely:** the 2025–26 Hormuz/Red Sea situation is live news; urgency is self-evident.
5. **Defensible by design:** the brief *wants* explicit, testable assumptions — so our transparent scenario engine turns the one hard-to-validate piece into a credibility feature.

## Positioning / narrative spine (for deck + video)
"In any sustained Hormuz disruption, India has ~9.5 days of cover and traditional planning tools can't model the shock, evaluate alternative corridors, or coordinate a response in real time. **Lodestar** watches the signals continuously, quantifies the disruption probability per corridor, simulates the cascade — supply gap → refinery run-rate → fuel price → SPR days → GDP — with assumptions you can see and change, and hands procurement teams a ranked, executable rerouting plan within minutes, not weeks."

## Lose-conditions to avoid
- Looking like a "speculative dashboard" (the exact critique leveled at weaker PS2 attempts) → counter with **live real data + an executable recommendation + a measured signal→recommendation latency**.
- Hidden/unjustifiable model assumptions → expose them as sliders.
- Overscoping to global coverage → depth on **Hormuz + India** is the demo spine; breadth is "future work."

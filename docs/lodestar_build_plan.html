00The bet

Why this can win, and what could lose it

PS2 is the rare statement where the demo can run on genuinely live public data — real tankers in the Strait of Hormuz, real Brent, real news — while sounding too hard for most teams to attempt. High perceived difficulty, low actual difficulty, near-empty field. That is the arbitrage.

We don't win by building "an AI dashboard." We win by building a decision tool: it watches signals continuously, quantifies disruption probability per corridor with assumptions you can see and change, and hands a procurement team a ranked, executable rerouting plan in minutes. The judging panel likely includes Octave (Hexagon spin-off) industrial-software people whose whole worldview is "turn fragmented operational data into decisive action, geospatially." We speak their language by design.

The one critique that kills us"Looks like a speculative dashboard." We pre-empt it with three things on screen at once: live real data, an executable recommendation, and a measured signal→recommendation latency. Every number traces to a source or a labelled, adjustable assumption — never a hidden constant.

03Architecture

How the pieces fit

A multi-agent backend orchestrated with LangGraph turns asynchronous feeds into one decision payload, streamed to a WebGL geospatial frontend. Decoupled, typed, and Dockerized so it runs with one command on demo day.

Flow: feeds → specialized agents (parallel) → deterministic engines do the math (agents reason about it, don't replace it) → orchestrator synthesizes → FastAPI streams a typed payload → deck.gl renders. The engines are deterministic on purpose: policymakers need auditability, not a black box.

06The three engines

Where the defensibility lives

Full math + every assumption value is in 04_model_assumptions_and_constants.md. The design principle across all three: deterministic, interpretable, adjustable. Agents reason about these outputs; they don't fabricate them.

1 · Risk score — explainable, not a black box INNOVATIONTECH

A sigmoid over a weighted sum of normalized features: P = sigmoid(β0 + Σ wᵢxᵢ), with β0 = −3.0 (≈4.7% resting probability). Features: kinetic news (0.40), AIS density anomaly (0.25), sanctions ratio (0.15), sea state (0.10), freight volatility (0.10). The UI shows each wᵢxᵢ as a stacked bar — that visible decomposition is our biggest "is it real" win.

2 · Scenario cascade — the cost of inaction BUSINESS IMPACT

Five deterministic steps: supply gap → refinery run-rate drop → SPR/buffer drawdown (days-of-cover countdown) → CPI delta (RBI rule: +10% crude ⇒ +0.3–0.4pp CPI) → GDP drag (~15bps) and CAD widening. Every assumption — Hormuz share, disruption %, substitution rate, SPR fill %, CPI sensitivity — is a labelled slider. The brief explicitly rewards explicit, testable assumptions, so we turn the one hard-to-validate piece into a credibility feature.

3 · Reroute MCDM — executable, grade-aware BUSINESS IMPACTINNOVATION

Ranks alternative crude sources by landed cost + voyage time + grade match − congestion. The grade-match matrix is the centrepiece: a cheap heavy-sour Venezuelan Merey cargo scores 0.0 for a simple PSU refinery that physically can't process it, and routes to RIL Jamnagar's coking units instead. That refinery-metallurgy constraint is why we beat a generic "cheapest barrel" recommender.

08Build timeline

Phases, not calendar days — compress or stretch to your sprint length. The rule: nothing in a later phase starts until the spine from Phase 1 renders something real.

  • Next.js + deck.gl + MapLibre; render live vessels in the Hormuz bbox.
  • GDELT connector + a minimal risk score (kinetic + density only).
  • Hard-coded scenario + reroute output, just to prove the pipe.

Exit test: real tankers move on screen and a corridor shows a live %.

P2ENGINES

  • Full risk model with all 5 features + per-feature stacked-bar breakdown. INNOV
  • Scenario cascade, 5 steps, every assumption wired to a slider. BIZ
  • Reroute MCDM + grade_match matrix from crude_grades.json. BIZ
  • Price connectors (EIA baseline + Alpha Vantage cached intraday).

Exit test: dragging "disruption %" changes the cascade and re-ranks reroutes.

P3AGENTS

Real orchestration, not a wrapper

  • LangGraph graph: Market · Logistics · Macro · Orchestrator nodes. TECH
  • Agents call the deterministic engines and narrate/justify outputs.
  • OpenSanctions vessel screening into the density/sanctions features. INNOV
  • Chroma RAG so the orchestrator can cite policy context. INNOV

Fallback: if the graph is flaky, run nodes sequentially — same outputs.

  • Map polish: risk polygons, refinery/SPR markers, dead-reckoning trails. UX

  • docker-compose one-command run + README + screenshots.

09Defensibility playbook

Answers ready before they ask

Assume an Octave expert who builds mission-critical software interrogates every claim. Rehearse these.

If they ask…You answer
"Is this data real or faked?"Live AISStream WebSocket — point at a vessel, read its MMSI, cross-check on any public AIS site. GDELT and Brent are live too.
"How did you validate the model?"It's interpretable by design — a sigmoid over auditable weights, not a black box. Here's the per-feature contribution on screen, and the resting probability is calibrated to ~4.7%.
"Aren't your economics made up?"Every assumption is a labelled slider sourced to RBI/ICRA heuristics and PPAC data. We don't claim predictive accuracy; we claim a transparent, testable what-if engine — which the brief explicitly rewards.
"Why would a refiner trust the reroute?"Because it respects refinery metallurgy — API gravity and sulfur grade-fit — not just price. A heavy-sour cargo won't be recommended to a refinery that can't process it.
"How is this different from Kpler/Vortexa?"They're descriptive — where oil is. We're prescriptive — sense → simulate macro impact → output an executable plan, modeled for India's specific constraints. Nobody closes that loop.
"Does it scale?"Corridors are config; deck.gl handles 20k+ vessels; agents are stateless. Hormuz is the demo depth; breadth is a config change.
10Demo script

3–4 minutes, no dead air

  • 0:00–0:25 — The stake. India imports ~90% of its crude; ~45–55% transits one strait; the SPR holds ~9.5 days. Traditional planning can't model a sustained Hormuz shock in real time. BIZ
  • 0:25–1:05 — Live sensing. Show real tankers moving in Hormuz. "This is live AIS, right now." Point to the corridor risk % and the per-feature breakdown — kinetic news, vessel density, sanctions. INNOV
  • 1:05–2:00 — Simulate the shock. Drag "disruption %" to 30% (the Q1-2026 figure). Watch days-of-cover fall, CPI and GDP move. "Every assumption here is visible and adjustable — sourced to RBI and PPAC." BIZ
  • 2:00–2:50 — The decision. The reroute panel ranks alternatives. Show why Urals→Jamnagar tops it and why Merey is filtered out for a PSU refinery. "This is an executable plan, grade-aware." INNOV
  • 2:50–3:20 — The proof. The latency badge: signal → recommendation in seconds, not weeks. "McKinsey found economies without automated rerouting took ~47 days longer to stabilize." TECH
  • 3:20–3:40 — Close. "Lodestar turns fragmented signals into decisive, anticipatory action across a critical supply chain." (Octave's own language — deliberate.)
Video constraintsMP4/MKV under 50MB, else a Google Drive link. Screen-record the live app; don't narrate over slides for the demo portion — judges want to see it run.
